About Me

I'm a research engineer specializing in computer vision, 3D graphics, and perceptual quality assessment. Currently, I'm a master's student at MTUCI (Radio Engineering, 2025–2027) and a researcher at the MTUCI Computer Vision Center, where I work on real-time XR production systems and 3D quality metrics.

In parallel, I work as a PS Core Engineer at Beeline, maintaining high‑load packet networks and system architecture. My research interests lie at the intersection of 3D asset quality evaluation, motion capture, and generative AI for 3D content.

I'm the author of the PLER metric — a novel full‑reference metric for evaluating geometric fidelity of 3D assets — and hold a registered software copyright for motion capture transfer to Unreal Engine.

Publications

PLER: A Novel Full-Reference Metric for Evaluating the Geometric Fidelity of 3D Assets
Gleb A. Voronkov, Andrei V. Balobanov
2026 Systems of Signals Generating and Processing in the Field of on Board Communications, IEEE, pp. 1–5, 2026
IEEE Xplore Abstract
This paper presents an enhancement of the PLER (Peak Length to Error Ratio) metric for objective quality assessment of 3D models. The original PLER metric, which compares a reference and test 3D model by analyzing ray distances from a bounding sphere, is improved with a new ray distribution method for more uniform coverage and higher accuracy.
Research of Methods for Modeling User Assessments of 3D Content for Extended Reality Systems
Gleb A. Voronkov, I.V. Vlasyuk
2025 Systems of Signals Generating and Processing in the Field of on Board Communications, IEEE, March 2025, pp. 1–5
IEEE Xplore Abstract
Analysis of methods for modeling user ratings of video sequences and their application to XR systems. Compares approaches for 2D and 3D content quality evaluation, with aim to develop a new method for 3D content assessment.
Error Analysis of Generating 3D Models of Objects Using Various Hardware Systems
Gleb A. Voronkov, V.Yu. Vinetsky, D.A. Egorov
IEEE Xplore, 2025
View on IEEE Abstract
Comparative analysis of photogrammetry, TrueDepth (FaceID), and LiDAR for creating 3D models. Includes classification of 3D objects by size to help select appropriate generation methods.
