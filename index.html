I help XR platforms, game studios, and 3D marketplaces answer one critical question: "How good is this 3D asset, objectively?"

My answer is PLER — a novel full‑reference metric for geometric fidelity of 3D assets, published in IEEE. It did not stop at theory: I engineered the entire evaluation framework, from mathematical formalization to a production‑ready Python API, validated across multiple generative pipelines.

This blend of algorithmic invention and system‑level engineering runs through all my work. I built the ARTCam virtual production studio (motion capture + photogrammetry + real‑time rendering), designed AI pipelines at Telcore, and operated high‑availability packet‑core networks at Beeline — a rare combination that lets me see both the mathematical beauty of a metric and the infrastructure required to deploy it at scale.

IEEE Xplore Scopus

PLER: A Novel Full‑Reference Metric for Evaluating the Geometric Fidelity of 3D Assets

Gleb A. Voronkov

We introduce PLER, a perceptually‑motivated metric that quantifies geometric distortion of 3D models relative to a reference. The metric is invariant to topology changes and robust to scale variations. Validated on ShapeNet and custom photogrammetry datasets.

Research of Methods for Modeling User Assessments of 3D Content for Extended Reality Systems

Vlasyuk I.V., Voronkov G.A.
2025 Systems of Signals Generating and Processing in the Field of on Board Communications, IEEE, 12 March 2025, pp. 1–5.

Comparative analysis of objective and subjective approaches to 3D quality assessment. We propose a hybrid model combining handcrafted geometric features with user‑perception weighting.

Error Analysis of Generating 3D Models of Objects Using Various Hardware Systems

Voronkov G.A. et al.

Systematic measurement of reconstruction errors for different depth‑sensing modalities (photogrammetry, LiDAR, TrueDepth). Introduces a taxonomy of 3D errors and recommends hardware‑metric pairings for industrial quality control.
